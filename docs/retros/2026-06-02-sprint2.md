### Proposed new lesson — L6 (secret-prefix literals in example/doc files)

**Trigger:** S6's `.env.example` comment used a literal `sk_live_…` to show the Tap
key shape; the S5 leak-guard regex (`/sk_live_|whsec_…/`) matched the *prefix itself*
and reddened the suite. The intent was documentation, not a real secret — but the
scanner can't tell, and neither can a reviewer skimming a diff.
**Rule:** in `.env.example`, `docs/`, comments, and test fixtures, never write a
real-looking secret **prefix** (`sk_live_`, `sk_test_`, `whsec_`, `pk_live_`,
`sb_secret_`, `Bearer eyJ…`). Use an abstract placeholder shape instead (`sk_…`,
`<tap-secret-key>`, `whsec_REDACTED`). Real secrets stay only in gitignored
`.env.local`.
**Effect on scoring:** Engineering hat capped at 8 for any story whose tracked files
contain a literal secret prefix, even in documentation/comment context.

→ **Add L6 to `.claude/lessons.md`?** (recommended — it's a concrete, recurring foot-gun)
