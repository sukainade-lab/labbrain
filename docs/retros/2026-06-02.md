## Focus findings (repeat issues, data-supported)

### 1. UX `<bdi>` on dynamic mixed-script content — **the #1 repeat miss**
UX scored 8 on first pass **twice**, both for the *same* root cause:
- **S2:** document filenames rendered without `<bdi>` → mixed Arabic/Latin SOP names mis-ordered.
- **S4:** dashboard counters + plan badge without `<bdi>` → numbers/Latin plan names mis-ordered inside RTL.

This is a MENA-specific RTL discipline gap. The fix each time was mechanical (wrap in `<bdi>`), but it cost a bridge round-trip both times. → **New lesson L5** (added to `.claude/lessons.md`).

### 2. Architecture capped at 8 by L3 on the last two stories
S4 and S5 both scored Architecture 8 on first pass purely because CI-green-on-PR wasn't yet confirmed at `/5-eo-score` time (L3 cap), then lifted to 10 at ship once `gh run` confirmed green. The cap is **working as designed** — it forces CI verification — but it costs a re-score round-trip every time. → **Process proposal P1** below (not a bug; a workflow optimization).

### 3. The "bridge to 100" pattern held all sprint
Every story landed first-pass at 80–96, then bridged honestly to 100. The bridge consistently closed: (a) end-to-end wiring gaps (L4/Product), (b) CI confirmation (L3/Arch), (c) RTL/`<bdi>` polish (UX). The discipline is sound; the goal for Sprint 2 is to **front-load** these so first-pass lands ≥90 without a bridge.

## Process proposals

- **P1 — Confirm CI green *before* `/5-eo-score`, not at `/7-eo-ship`.** L3 caps Architecture at 8 until CI-green-on-PR is confirmed. Opening the PR and confirming CI at the end of `/4-eo-review` (instead of at ship) would let Architecture score on merit at `/5-eo-score` and eliminate the 96→100 re-score round-trip that happened on S4 and S5. Candidate refinement to the `/4-eo-review` → `/5-eo-score` handoff. *(Not added as a lesson — it's a workflow tweak, and L3 already encodes the underlying rule.)*

- **P2 (tooling, for `/8-eo-retro` → SOP) — `eo-brain-ingester` parser regex false negatives.** During bootstrap the parser scored the BRD 4/10 and mis-carved the MVP because its regex expects `### Story N` headers and `[loop:x]` tags, while the BRD legitimately uses `### S1 — …` headers with spaced `[loop: auth, compliance]` tags. The real tags were already present; the parser's proposed re-carve (freezing S5 into Phase-2) was wrong and was correctly overridden at bootstrap. **Proposed SOP fix:** widen the ingester regex to recognize `S\d+` headers and `\[loop:\s*...\]` with internal spaces. Belongs in `skills/eo-brain-ingester/` — surface upstream; no local code change.
